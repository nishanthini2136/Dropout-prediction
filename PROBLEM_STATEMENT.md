# Problem Statement

## Title

**A Hybrid ML-DL Framework for Early Dropout Prediction and Personalized Learning Interventions in E-Learning Systems**

The rapid growth of e-learning platforms has revolutionized education by providing flexible, accessible, and self-paced learning opportunities to students worldwide. Despite these advantages, student dropout remains a significant challenge that affects learning outcomes, course completion rates, and the overall effectiveness of online education systems. Identifying students who are at risk of dropping out at an early stage is crucial for providing timely support and improving learner retention.

Existing dropout prediction systems primarily rely on traditional machine learning techniques and learner behavioral data such as quiz performance, assignment completion, video engagement, and forum participation. While these approaches achieve reasonable prediction accuracy, they often focus only on current dropout risk and provide static recommendations that do not adapt to changing student behavior. Furthermore, they lack the ability to effectively capture complex temporal patterns and evolving engagement trends present in student learning activities.

To address these limitations, this project proposes a **Hybrid Machine Learning and Deep Learning (ML-DL) Framework** for early dropout prediction and personalized learning interventions in e-learning environments. The framework combines the strengths of machine learning models for feature-based prediction with deep learning techniques capable of learning hidden patterns from sequential learner interactions.

The proposed system aims to:

* Predict potential student dropout at an early stage using historical and real-time learning behavior data.
* Analyze temporal engagement patterns through deep learning models to improve prediction accuracy.
* Forecast future dropout risk trends to enable proactive intervention.
* Generate personalized learning roadmaps tailored to individual student needs, weaknesses, and learning progress.
* Provide adaptive intervention strategies that continuously evolve based on student responses and engagement outcomes.

By integrating early dropout prediction, risk forecasting, personalized learning support, and adaptive intervention mechanisms into a unified framework, the proposed system seeks to enhance student engagement, improve course completion rates, reduce dropout rates, and contribute to a more effective and intelligent e-learning ecosystem.
